Features
Paste or Open a Song
Paste chord/lyric text, open a file (.txt, .docx, .cho, .fba), or try the demo. Chords go on the line above lyrics.
Transpose with Shapes + Capo
Pick the Sounding Key the audience hears, then the Shape Key your fingers play. Capo auto-calculates. Or nudge capo manually.
♯ Nashville Numbers
Toggle to replace chords with scale-degree numbers (1, 4, 5⁻, etc.). Numbers stay the same in every key.
✎ Edit Chord Positions
Click the button, click any chord, then arrow keys or Space/Backspace to slide it over the correct syllable.
Chord Diagrams & Simplified Voicings
Each chord shows a fretboard diagram. Common chords include a simpler alternative that's easier to play.
💾 Save & Reopen
Save as .fba — stores song, settings, and position edits. Reopen from the Open File tab.
🖨 Smart Print Preview
Auto-fits to fewest pages. Adjust columns, font size (9–12pt), spacing. Optionally include chord diagrams.
▶ Performance Mode
Fullscreen chord sheet with adjustable auto-scroll speed and optional metronome. Access from the ☰ menu.
How To Use
Getting Started
1. Paste your chord sheet into the input area, or open a file (.txt, .docx, .cho).
2. Click Parse & Load Song.
3. Pick your favorite chord shapes under Play Shapes In.
4. Adjust the Sounding Key to match the song — the capo calculates for you.
5. Fine-tune chord positions with ✎ Edit Positions if needed.
6. Save your work with 💾 Save .fba or print with 🖨 Print.
Performance Mode
1. Load and set up your song first.
2. Click the ▶ button (or find it in the ☰ menu).
3. Adjust scroll speed with the +/− buttons before you start.
4. Hit ▶ Play — a 15-second countdown gives you time to grab your guitar.
5. Turn on the Metronome toggle and set your BPM if you want a click track.
6. Hit ⏸ Pause anytime, or ✕ Exit to return.
Nashville Number System
Highlighted columns (1, 4, 5, 6m) are the most common chord combinations in popular music. Minor chords are marked with "m".
